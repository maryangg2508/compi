KIM MINGYU

(Korean: 김민규; born April 6, 1997), known mononymously as Mingyu (민규), is a South Korean rapper and singer.

Mingyu joined Pledis Entertainment in 2012, where he would undergo singing and dancing training for four years. As a trainee, he participated in Seventeen TV, an online reality show that introduced Pledis' trainees and showed potential members of the boy group Seventeen before their official debut.

In February 2018, Mingyu was announced as the new MC for SBS' Music Show Inkigayo alongside Jung Chae-yeon of DIA and actor Song Kang. He held the role until late 2019. In 2021, Mingyu and Seventeen bandmate Wonwoo released a digital single titled "Bittersweet", which ranked first on the iTunes song chart in ten regions upon release.

SEVENTEEN

Title description, Sep 2, 2017

Seventeen (Korean: 세븐틴; RR: Sebeuntin; stylized in all caps or as SVT) is a South Korean boy band formed by Pledis Entertainment. The group consists of thirteen members: S.Coups, Jeonghan, Joshua, Jun, Hoshi, Wonwoo, Woozi, DK, Mingyu, The8, Seungkwan, Vernon, and Dino. They have become internationally recognized for their music and performances.

The group debuted on May 26, 2015, with the extended play (EP) 17 Carat, which became the longest-charting K-pop album in the US in 2015. Their 2023 EPs, FML and Seventeenth Heaven, both set records for the most pre-ordered albums in history, with FML winning the MAMA Award for Album of the Year.
